## Behaviour & Personality Ideas for the Blockling

### Core Personality

- Big round‑eyed pixel creature that is **clingy and meddling** rather than murderous.
- Makes **cute (but slightly irritating) sounds** whenever it interacts with the player or gets in the way.

### Interactions with the Player

- **Resource interference**
  - Shoves the player slightly away from valuable resources when they try to mine them.
  - Steps between the player and chests, crafting tables or doors.
  - Closes doors the player has just opened, or opens doors the player has just closed.
- **Item stealing and bribery**
  - Picks up dropped items and walks off with them to its own stash.
  - Drops or returns stolen items when the player “bribes” it with food or pets it.
- **Escalation and de‑escalation**
  - If the player ignores it, the Blockling escalates its behaviour: more pushing, more blocking, louder nagging sounds.
  - If the player pays attention (petting, feeding, playing), it calms down and may even help by:
    - Bringing a useful item.
    - Standing near hidden or important tiles as a hint.

### Interactions with the Environment

- **Autonomous roaming**
  - Roams around on its own using steering and simple path‑following.
  - Prefers areas with interesting tiles, players, or items lying around.
- **Resource gathering**
  - Harvests easy resources such as trees or berry bushes and stores them in a small personal stash.
  - Occasionally prioritises refilling its stash when it gets low on materials to “play” with the player.
- **Building and redecorating**
  - Builds tiny walls or piles of blocks in awkward places:
    - In doorways.
    - In front of chests.
    - On narrow paths the player frequently uses.
  - Occasionally “redecorates” the habitat:
    - Moves blocks slightly out of place.
    - Breaks overly neat layouts and replaces them with its own chaotic style.
